# Sample Audit Output

Overall risk: High

Flag count: 7

Categories fired:
- Formulaic contrast
- Condensed expert prose
- Lifeless verbs and decorative adjectives
- Empty generalization
- Redundancy and over-explanation
- Lexical tells

## Findings

1. Formulaic contrast

Excerpt:
> This is not just a documentation process, it is a comprehensive operating model

Why it fires:
The contrast tries to elevate the idea, but the second half is abstract and
larger-sounding rather than clearer.

Suggested rewrite:
> The documentation process gives leaders one place to see decisions, risks,
> owners, and delivery status.

2. Condensed expert prose

Excerpt:
> portfolio governance, stakeholder alignment, decision cadence, executive visibility, delivery readiness, risk mitigation, and cross-functional accountability

Why it fires:
The sentence packs seven concepts into one list. It sounds knowledgeable, but it
asks the reader to unpack the operating model instead of showing how it works.

Suggested rewrite:
> It tracks the decisions leaders need to make, the risks that need ownership,
> and the delivery work that is ready to move.

3. Empty generalization

Excerpt:
> In today's rapidly evolving business landscape

Why it fires:
The phrase delays the point and could fit almost any business topic.

Suggested rewrite:
Delete it.

4. Lifeless verbs and lexical tells

Excerpts:
> leverages
> robust and scalable
> streamline
> optimize
> drive meaningful outcomes

Why it fires:
These phrases sound polished but do not name concrete action or evidence.

Suggested rewrite:
Use direct verbs such as shows, tracks, reduces, routes, or decides.
